Capturing a URL

ReservesDirect allows you to link to any content on the Internet that can be opened in a web browser, including web pages, documents, and content available through electronic journals and databases that Emory subscribes to.

Always remember to include the "http://" prefix in any link you add.

When linking to content in an electronic journal or database that the library subscribes to, be sure to select a "persistent" or permanent URL and not one that contains session IDs or other identifiers that cause the URL to time out or otherwise expire. In many cases, you will not be able to simple cut and paste the URL out of your browser window. For assistance in finding stable URLs, contact reserves staff at your library.

Describing The URL

The most basic descriptors for a URL are title and, if applicable, author or entity responsible for creating or maintaining it. If the link is a journal article or book chapter, you may also fill in bibliographic information in the additional fields.

Availability of Materials

Links that you create are instantly available to your students, as long as the link is stable and target web site is available.
